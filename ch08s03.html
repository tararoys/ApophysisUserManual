Moving with Post Transforms

select one of your transforms, go to the top of your transform editor, and click on the post transform button,

A new triangle will appear on your screen. Click on it, shape it, and move it about.

Hey! The sinusoidal is moving and changing shape! So what happened? You are moving the post transform. Since the post transform moves and reshapes the fractal after the variation is added, the sinusoidal can actually be manipulated and moved about. Let me illustrate this with more pretty pictures.

All right. Here is a transform. You can move it about, twist it, and do whatever to it. Since there's only one transform, it doesn't do very interesting things, but oh well.

Here is the transform with the sinusoidal variation added. No matter what position the original transform was in, the sinusoidal variation will take that transform and confine it to a square.

Finally, here is a three transform with a post transform added. This transform controls the fractals movement after the variation is added, so you can take that square and move it about the screen.

Now, recall MRCM. MRCM creates the fractal by using each transform to make a copy of the whole fractal. Every time the whole fractal is copied, three things are calculated on each transform in this order: the original transform movements are calculated, the variation is calculated, and the post transform is calculated. Got all that? Good! Now I'll show you a few fun tricks you can do with post transforms.
