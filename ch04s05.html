Gradient Rotation

Now, gradient rotation! Open up the gradient dialog.

Click on the slider and move it around. The colors on the fractal begin moving, right? Right. What's happening? Remember those arrows pointing from the red triangle to the gradient? Suppose that gradient is slid to the left. The arrows point to different colors, yes? So, when we rotate a gradient, we cause the triangles in the fractal to look up different colors.

All right, let's move on to a more interesting fractal. Put the upper left-hand transform's color slider back to zero- in other words, move it all the way to the left. Now, go to the lower left-hand color slider and set it to one- in other words, move it all the way to the right.

Personally, I love this quilt. And it brings us to coloring rule number three: having a balanced color scheme rocks. The two diagonal transforms are colored, and this is reflected all throughout the quilt. So, oftentimes, if you have two transforms that are about the same size and would make a good symmetry coloring, you should probably set both of their color sliders to 1.

More Gradient Options

This fractal also gives me a good excuse to teach you a bit more about the options on the gradient menu . Bring it back up.

Click on rotate. A small menu with several options should come up.

Select Hue and move the slider. Hue changes the color of the gradient. Saturation changes how intense or dull the colors are. Brightness controls how bright or dark the colors get. Contrast controls well, contrast- that is, how bright or dark the colors are when compared to one another. Blur blurs the gradient together to get a simpler fractal. Frequency increases or decreases how often the gradient repeats its pattern. I'd recommend playing with all of these. It's fun!
