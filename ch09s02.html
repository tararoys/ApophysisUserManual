How Final Transforms Work

What is the final transform? The final transform only comes into play after the entire fractal has already been calculated. The final transform is a giant variation that is applied once, and only once, to the entire fractal. Let me explain using pretty pictures.

Here is a fractal without any post transform applied.

below is the grid that a post transform with a sinusoidal variation on it. The final transform was added by pushing the final transform button

. The sinusoidal variation does not allow fractals to go beyond a certain square area. By adding a sinusoidal variation to the final transform, we basically told Apophysis, "Make a fractal, and then cram it into a square and fold over whatever edges don't fit."

Now, look at the pictures below. On top is the fractal, on the bottom is the transform editor corresponding to this fractal. Look at the transform editor. The final transform is shown in white, and the white grid that represents the sinusoidal variation. The whole fractal is drawn on that grid. In essence, the final transform squished the whole fractal so that it fit inside that grid.

So, the final transform gives you an opportunity to distort the fractal as a whole. Get that? Good! Adding different variations to the final transform will make strange and different grids to draw the whole fractal on. Experiment! Lots of cool things can be done with the final transform.
