How Variables Work

Here's another example of final transforms that will allow me to show you one of the features of Apophysis that we haven't covered yet: variables.

I will use the final transform to introduce you to variables and to teach you how to make a heart.

Go to the variations tab in your transform editor, and scroll all the way down to the bottom, until you encounter a bunch of variations highlighted in blue. These are special variations, because they are controlled by variables.

What are variables? In your transform editor, click on the variables tab. You will see a list of rather confusing names and numbers. These are variables, and they control how the blue-highlighted variations work. Most of them correspond to a certain variation: for example, the radial blur angle controls how the radial blur variation acts. I don't know what exactly all of the variables do. Play, and you're figure it out.
